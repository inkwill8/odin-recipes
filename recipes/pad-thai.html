Traditional Pad Thai

Description

Pad Thai is a popular Thai stir-fry dish made with rice noodles, vegetables, and your choice of protein. It's a flavorful and filling meal that's quick to make.

  • 1 cup rice noodles
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 small onion, thinly sliced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 shallot, thinly sliced
  • 2 tablespoons tamarind paste
  • 2 tablespoons fish sauce
  • 1 tablespoon palm sugar
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup bean sprouts
  • 1/4 cup chopped peanuts
  • 1 pound protein (shrimp, chicken, tofu)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Lime wedges, chopped cilantro, or diced chilies for topping

Steps

  1. Heat the vegetable oil in a large wok or frying pan over medium-high heat. Add the onion, garlic, and shallot and stir-fry until softened.
  2. Add the tamarind paste, fish sauce, palm sugar, and soy sauce. Stir to combine and cook for 1-2 minutes.
  3. Add the cooked noodles, bean sprouts, and peanuts to the pan. Stir-fry everything together until the noodles are well coated with the sauce.
  4. Add your choice of protein and stir-fry until cooked through. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  5. Transfer the Pad Thai to a serving platter or individual plates. Serve hot, garnished with lime wedges, chopped cilantro, or diced chilies.
